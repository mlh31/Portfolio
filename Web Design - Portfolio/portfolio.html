--ILLUSTATION DESIGN
Illustration Design
This illustration design is part of my collection to create a successful festival poster. I began this project by researching which type of festival poster I wanted to create that would express how I, as a designer, wanted to represent my skill set. The best way I found to do this was to look at my favourite type of graphic design work and created a mood board, the images I chose all correlated perfectly with a fashion festival so this is the festival choice I chose.

For this specific piece I wanted to create a bold graphic that would capture a viewer’s attention and appeal to the correct target audience, to do this I experimented with different colour variations, compositions and illustrations until I created something I thought represented fashion nicely as well as conveying a strong, bold theme made to attract my target audience of young women. I added some details like the festival name, the location, website and Facebook details to give the reader more information on the event and played with different hierarchy’s to ensure the information was conveyed properly and important information stood out.
--Magazine Design
Magazine
This project involved creating a professional magazine cover that tailors to a large target audience. I wanted to create a magazine that I could imagine being sold in shops and online worldwide. To do this, I started by looking at conventions and connotations of successful magazines that exist for similar target audiences, from this, I decided to place my model in front of the title as this is a common feature in famous magazines, such as Vogue, Elle and many other fashion magazines that tailor to the same target audience. I also believe that it draws the viewers’ attention into the model.

I include interesting and different typefaces for segments that could be found within the magazine and edited the foliage in the background of the image to appear three-dimensional. This was to help with the overall hierarchy of the page. I also placed my model directly in the middle of the page with her eyes looking at the viewer. This helps to draw viewers in and want to learn more. In order to create this design, I made use of Photoshop, especially layering to ensure the dimensional aspects where incorporated correctly.
--POSTER DESIGN
Poster Dresign
This project’s brief was to create an engaging festival poster for a decided target audience, I chose to create a fashion festival poster aimed at anyone from the age of 18 to 30. The reason I chose this age range was based on my research into the fashion industry at the general age range that is both inspired by fashion festivals and attend fashion festivals.

For this project, I made use of abstract shape and symmetry to create a vibrant and colourful feel whilst maintaining some form of order within the piece. I wanted to combine two opposites together to make a juxtaposing form of art created to entice the viewer to view this poster and to want to learn more about the festival being advertised. My process when creating this piece was more experimental than some of my other graphic design work, I began by starting to create a face, and decided this gave a good generalised shape, I then experimented by flipping this and having two versions of the same image facing each other. I then began adding random shapes and playing with the colour palette until I was happy, I then placed all of this inside of a box to frame the design and added some details about the festival.
--Photo Manipulation
Photo Manipulation
This project challenged my Photoshop skills and helped me to think outside of the box when experimenting with photo manipulation.

I experimented with many different ideas before settling on my theme of 'mother nature', but this process of trial and error helped me when expanding my knowledge with Photoshop. In all of my experimentations it was evident that a human figure was somewhat an importance in each, so I decided to manipulate images I had already collected and experiment with these. I made use of colour correcting to make sure the image had a good balance and didn't feel too unrealistic.

I also played around with opacity and placed some bark going up the model's neck, this helped with the overall illusion that I wanted to represent. I felt that this project was extremely helpful to my personal development and technical skills and overall the image turned out fairly well. Photo manipulation is a powerful storytelling tool and it's a real asset when thinking about how to use my images in a different way.
--POSTER DESIGN
Poster design
This poster design is one of my favourite graphic design pieces that I made. The project was to create a successful poster to promote an up and coming fashion festival.

My process began by initially creating the main graphic of the character that would be the branding of the festival. Once I was happy with my character design, I began attempting to find the right colours and compositions to promote my festival whilst including all the relevant information. In order to do this, I wanted to create a title that fit my festival theme, was interesting and would look good as a brand. My main concern once working with this font was how I got the title to interact with the character, I originally wanted to have the font flowing with the outline of her hair, but soon found this to be very difficult to accomplish. I the began to look at the rule of thirds, here, I decided that the most effective way to visually portray my poster was to keep my character primarily on the left-hand side, with the title on the right and the information, the centre right. When I asked my peers what they thought of this design, they seemed to really like the idea behind it and the font worked well sitting next to the graphics. Overall this was my favourite design for this project.
--POSTER DESIGN
Layout Dresign
This was a self-led project, I wanted to create a poster that was unlike any of my others and experiment with different compositing and to enhance my technical skills when working with Photoshop and InDesign. I made use of clipping masks and layering to get my graphics in the correct place, I used a gradient on the page to add some depth and contrasting colours to make the poster more appealing. I envisioned this poster advertising a band's performance or a similar event and I think the layout works well and could be used for a variety of different poster designs.

When I was creating the astronaut graphics for this piece I tried to encorporate texture into my design, to do this, I photographed and scanned in different pieces of tin foil and made use of clipping masks to get these to fit into where I wanted them. I think this technique really enhanced the graphics and makes them stand out, with the intention to draw in a viewer.
--Colour Experimentation
Colour Experimentation
This is an example of colour experimentation that I created when creating an illustration design, I really liked this style of colour experimentation as I could see how different colours worked well together and it made it easy to see the contrast between the character and the background image. This was a big part of my process when designing and it helped me pick and choose what works well and what doesn't. --POSTER DESIGN
Poster design
For this project, I used Processing and Arduino to create my own, interactive game. The concept behind the game was to shoot your opponents from your spaceship to win, it had 10 different levels that progressed in difficulty as the game did and a boss level at the end where the boss attempts to shoot you as well, and you have to avoid the shots in order to win.

This was an extremely insightful project, but I particularly enjoyed creating the game graphics for the game, creating pixelated spaceships was a difficult task, but taught me the best ways to work with pixels in Photoshop. The different coloured spaceships represented different levels, for instance, if you hit a red spaceship, it would turn yellow, then green and so forth. I also incorporated bonus health and point icons that the user could collect when playing the game.

3-D MODELLING

This project was to create 3-D elements inside a photographed frame and to make the 3-D element look realistic by manipulating the camera and lighting in Maya. This project was particularly challenging and was my first experience working in a 3-D modelling software. The main aim was to recreate the lights and camera within the room in a platform and apply textures and colours to the elements to make them seem realistic.
I am particularly proud of how this project turned out as I found it so challenging and learnt a new program in the process.
